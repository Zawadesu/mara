CYP2C9 catalyzes NADPH/Oâ‚‚-dependent oxidation of endogenous and xenobiotic compounds. It epoxidizes polyunsaturated fatty acids and hydroxylates carbon-hydrogen bonds, notably converting cholesterol to 25-hydroxycholesterol (a cholesterol homeostasis regulator) and weakly metabolizing estrogens to catechol forms. It also oxygenates plant monoterpenes (e.g., limonene to carveol/perillyl alcohol) and is a key enzyme in drug metabolism, contributing to the pharmacokinetic variability of S-warfarin, diclofenac, phenytoin, tolbutamide, and losartan.
